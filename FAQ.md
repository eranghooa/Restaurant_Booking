## About the Restaurant

### Q: What is 雅庭 Miyabi-tei?
**A:** 雅庭 Miyabi-tei (pronounced "mee-yah-bee tay") is a Japanese fine dining restaurant in Singapore. The name means "elegant garden" in Japanese. Established in 1994, it is a sanctuary of Japanese hospitality — *omotenashi* — where every detail, from the clay of each vessel to the temperature of the dashi, is composed with quiet devotion.

### Q: What cuisine does Miyabi-tei serve?
**A:** Miyabi-tei serves Japanese fine dining with a focus on the *omakase* tradition — a chef-led multi-course experience where dishes change with the season. The menu follows the Japanese philosophy of *shun* (旬), the peak of seasonal perfection. Ingredients are sourced daily from Tsukiji market and Kyoto's finest producers.

### Q: When was Miyabi-tei established?
**A:** Miyabi-tei was established in 1994 in Singapore.

### Q: What is the concept behind Miyabi-tei?
**A:** Miyabi-tei is built on the Japanese philosophy of *omotenashi* (unconditional hospitality) and *shun* (seasonal harmony). Every element of the dining experience — the ceramics, the dashi temperature, the course sequence, the *ma* (pause) between courses — is composed with deliberate intention. The itamae (master chef) crafts each dish to honour nature's seasonal peak.

## Dining Experience

### Q: What is *omakase*?
**A:** *Omakase* (おまかせ) means "I leave it up to you" in Japanese. It is a chef-led dining format where the itamae (master chef) selects and prepares a sequence of seasonal dishes for the guest. The menu changes with the season and the day's best ingredients, offering a curated journey rather than ordering from a fixed menu.

### Q: What is *shun*?
**A:** *Shun* (旬) is the Japanese concept of seasonal perfection — the precise moment when an ingredient is at its absolute peak in flavour, texture, and freshness. At Miyabi-tei, all dishes are composed around *shun*, which is why the menu changes with the seasons.

### Q: What is *omotenashi*?
**A:** *Omotenashi* (おもてなし) is the Japanese philosophy of unconditional, wholehearted hospitality. It goes beyond service — it is an anticipation of the guest's needs before they are expressed. It is the spirit that underpins every aspect of the Miyabi-tei experience.

### Q: What is *ma* and how does it relate to dining at Miyabi-tei?
**A:** *Ma* (間) is a Japanese concept describing meaningful pause and negative space. At Miyabi-tei, *ma* is observed in the thoughtful intervals between courses, the quiet of the room, and the deliberate pacing of the meal — all crafted so the guest can fully appreciate each dish before the next arrives.

### Q: What is an *itamae*?
**A:** An *itamae* (板前) is a Japanese master chef, traditionally associated with sushi and kaiseki. At Miyabi-tei, the itamae crafts each piece of nigiri and seasonal dish with the trained precision of years of apprenticeship — including techniques such as scoring hamo pike conger with 120 cuts per inch.
